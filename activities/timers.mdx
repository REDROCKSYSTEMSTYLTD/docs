Timers let you track time as you work rather than calculating hours after the fact. When you stop a timer, CoordHub converts it into a pre-filled activity form.

## Starting a timer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Quick Actions">
    Click **Quick Actions** in the header and select **Start a Timer**. Or go to **Activities → Timers** and click **New Timer**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the participant">
    Choose the participant this session is for. The timer starts running immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let it run">
    The timer keeps running while you work. You can navigate anywhere in CoordHub and the timer continues in the background. The header shows a small timer indicator while it's active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing active timers

Go to **Activities → Timers** to see all your running timers. You can run multiple timers simultaneously if you're switching between participants during a session.

From the Timers view you can:
- **Pause** a timer (keeps the elapsed time)
- **Resume** a paused timer
- **Stop** a timer and convert it to an activity

## Converting a timer to an activity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stop the timer">
    Click **Stop** on the timer. CoordHub shows you the total elapsed time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and complete the activity form">
    The activity form opens with the participant, date, and duration pre-filled from the timer. Add the support item and write your case note.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save or submit">
    Save as draft or submit for approval as you normally would.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you forget to stop a timer, you can manually adjust the end time on the activity form before submitting. The timer's start time is locked, but you can edit the end time.
</Tip>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Common questions">
    **What happens if I forget to stop a timer?**
    The timer keeps running indefinitely until you stop it manually. When you eventually stop it and open the activity form, you'll see the full elapsed duration — including any overnight or weekend time if you forgot for that long. Edit the end time on the activity form before submitting to correct the duration to what you actually worked.

    **Can I edit the time after stopping a timer?**
    Yes — when you stop a timer, the activity form opens with the start time locked (matching when you started the timer) and the end time editable. Change the end time to correct the duration. Once you submit the activity for approval, the duration is locked with the rest of the activity fields.

    **Can I run timers for multiple participants at once?**
    Yes — CoordHub supports multiple simultaneous timers. Each timer is linked to a specific participant. Go to Activities → Timers to see all your running timers. Be careful with multiple timers — make sure you're stopping the right one when you finish a session.

    **Do timers save if I close my browser?**
    Yes — timers are saved server-side, not in your browser. If you close the tab, restart your computer, or lose connectivity, the timer continues running. Reopen CoordHub and go to Activities → Timers to find it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
