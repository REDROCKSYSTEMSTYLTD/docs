Invoices in CoordHub are built from approved activities. You don't manually enter line items — you select the activities you want to bill and CoordHub assembles the invoice.

## Before you start

Activities must be **Approved** before they can be invoiced. If you have activities in Draft or Pending status, get them approved first.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Finance → Invoices">
    In the sidebar, click **Finance**, then **Invoices**. Click **New Invoice** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the participant">
    Start typing the participant's name. CoordHub shows matching participants with approved, un-invoiced activities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select activities to include">
    CoordHub lists all approved activities for this participant that haven't been invoiced yet. Tick the ones you want to include in this invoice.

    Each activity shows the date, support item, duration, and amount. Check the amounts match what you expect before continuing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the invoice">
    CoordHub builds the invoice preview. Check:
    - Correct participant name and NDIS number
    - Correct invoice date (defaults to today — change if needed)
    - All line items are present and correct
    - The recipient — for plan-managed participants, confirm the plan manager details
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invoice">
    Click **Send Invoice** to finalise and dispatch. For plan-managed participants, this sends the invoice to the plan manager. For NDIA-managed participants, the activities are queued for PRODA export instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Returning an invoice to draft

If you need to correct an invoice after creating it but before sending, click **Return to Draft** on the invoice detail page. Once an invoice has been sent, contact your administrator — they can return it to draft if needed.

<Warning>
  Once an invoice is sent, the activities it contains are locked — they can't be added to another invoice or returned to the billing queue without an administrator returning the invoice to draft first.
</Warning>
