## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Providers in the sidebar">
    Click **Providers** (or **Directory** depending on your sidebar layout) to open the provider list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Provider">
    Click **Add Provider** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter provider details">
    Fill in:
    - **Organisation name**
    - **ABN** — the provider's Australian Business Number
    - **NDIS registration number** (if they're a registered NDIS provider)
    - **Contact name, phone, and email** — your main point of contact at this organisation
    - **Service categories** — the NDIS support categories this provider delivers
    - **Address and suburb** — used for location-based matching
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider appears in the directory and can be linked to participants.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Linking a provider to a participant

Once a provider is in the directory:
1. Open the participant profile and go to the **Providers** tab
2. Click **Link Provider**
3. Search for and select the provider
4. Choose the support category this provider delivers for this participant

<Tip>
  If a provider serves many of your participants, you only need to add them to the directory once. Link them individually to each participant from the Providers tab on the participant's profile.
</Tip>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Common questions">
    **What if the provider isn't NDIS registered?**
    Unregistered providers can still be added to the CoordHub directory. Leave the NDIS Registration Number field blank. Note: for NDIA-managed participants, supports can only be delivered by registered providers. For plan-managed or self-managed participants, unregistered providers may be used. If you're linking an unregistered provider to an NDIA-managed participant's record, document the reason.

    **Can one provider be linked to multiple participants?**
    Yes — add the provider once to the directory, then link them to as many participants as needed from each participant's Providers tab. The provider record is shared; the participant link is individual.

    **What if two organisations have the same or similar name?**
    Add both as separate provider records and use the ABN field to distinguish them — ABNs are unique identifiers for Australian businesses. Add a note in the provider record (e.g. "[Organisation name] — [suburb] branch") to make them easier to tell apart.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
