Contacts in CoordHub are the people connected to a participant — formally and informally. Having accurate contact records means you always know who to reach, who has legal authority, and who to involve in decision-making.

## Contact types

CoordHub supports 7 contact roles:

| Role | Who this is |
|---|---|
| **Guardian** | A person or organisation with legal decision-making authority (e.g. OPA WA-appointed guardian) |
| **Nominee** | A person formally appointed by the NDIA to act on the participant's behalf |
| **Advocate** | A disability advocate or advocacy organisation |
| **Legal Representative** | A lawyer or other legal representative |
| **Emergency Contact** | Who to call in an emergency — does not have formal authority |
| **GP / Allied Health** | The participant's general practitioner or treating clinician |
| **Other** | Any other relevant contact |

## Where contacts appear

Contacts added here are available throughout CoordHub — they're referenced in service agreements, intake assessments, and NDIA progress reports. The **Representatives** tab on the participant profile is specifically for formal representatives (guardians, nominees, advocates) who have legal authority documents attached.

<Info>
  Plan managers are managed separately under Finance → Plan Managers, not in the participant's Contacts tab. This is because plan managers are linked to specific budget categories, not to the participant as a whole.
</Info>
